The Last Tree

The last tree stood in the middle of a barren field. It was a lone survivor of a forest that had been cut down years ago. The tree was old and gnarled, and its branches were twisted and bent. But it was still alive, and it still stood tall.

One day, a young boy came to the field. He was playing in the dirt when he saw the tree. He had never seen a tree so old and so big before. He walked up to the tree and touched its bark. It was rough and hard, but it was also warm.

The boy sat down at the foot of the tree and looked up at the branches. He thought about all the other trees that had been cut down. He wondered why this tree had been left standing.

The boy sat there for a long time, just thinking. He thought about the forest that had once been here. He thought about the animals that had lived in the forest. He thought about the people who had come to the forest to play and to gather wood.

The boy closed his eyes and listened to the wind blowing through the tree's branches. He could hear the sound of birds singing in the distance. He felt the sun on his face and the wind in his hair.

The boy opened his eyes and looked up at the tree again. He smiled. He knew that the tree was a symbol of hope. It was a reminder that even in the midst of destruction, there is always hope for life.

The boy stood up and walked away. He knew that he would never forget the tree. It had taught him a valuable lesson about hope.
